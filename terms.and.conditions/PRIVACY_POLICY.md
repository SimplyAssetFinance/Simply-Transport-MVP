## 4. How We Store Your Information

### 4.1 Data Location

All data is stored in Australia using Supabase infrastructure located in the Sydney region (ap-southeast-2). Your data does not leave Australia except as described in Section 6 (Third Party Services).

### 4.2 Security Measures

We protect your data with:

- **Encryption at rest:** AES-256 encryption for all database content
- **Encryption in transit:** TLS 1.3 for all data transfers
- **Access controls:** Row Level Security ensures you can only access your organisation's data
- **Authentication:** Secure password hashing, optional two-factor authentication
- **Audit logging:** All actions are logged for security monitoring
- **Private document storage:** Uploaded files are stored in private buckets, not publicly accessible

### 4.3 Password Security

Passwords are hashed using industry-standard algorithms. We never store passwords in plain text and our staff cannot view your password.

## 7. Your Rights

Under the Australian Privacy Principles, you have the right to:

### 7.1 Access Your Information

You can request a copy of all personal information we hold about you. We will respond within 30 days.

**How to request:** Email [support email] with subject "Data Access Request"

### 7.2 Correct Your Information

You can update your information through the Settings page, or contact us if you need assistance.

### 7.3 Delete Your Information

You can request deletion of your account and all associated data.

**Process:**
1. Request deletion via Settings > Account > Delete Account, or email us
2. We will disable your account immediately
3. Data is held for 30 days (in case you change your mind)
4. After 30 days, data is permanently deleted

**Note:** Audit logs are retained for 7 years for legal compliance, even after account deletion.

### 7.4 Data Portability

You can export your data at any time via Settings > Export Data. Exports are provided in CSV format.

### 7.5 Withdraw Consent

Where we rely on consent (e.g., marketing emails), you can withdraw at any time by:
- Clicking "Unsubscribe" in any email
- Updating preferences in Settings > Notifications
- Contacting us directly

### 7.6 Complain

If you believe we have breached the Privacy Act, you can:
1. Contact us first — we want to resolve issues directly
2. Lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at [oaic.gov.au](https://www.oaic.gov.au)

## 8. Data Breach Response

In the event of an eligible data breach (as defined by the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme), we will:

1. **Contain** the breach immediately
2. **Assess** whether personal information was accessed or exfiltrated
3. **Notify the OAIC** within 72 hours if the breach is likely to result in serious harm
4. **Notify affected individuals** as soon as practicable with:
   - Description of the breach
   - Type of information involved
   - Steps we are taking
   - Steps you should take
   - Contact details for more information
